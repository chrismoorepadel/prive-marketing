# Privé Padel — Brand Standards (Phase Two)

This document is the source of truth for all brand decisions in this codebase. When in doubt, defer to this file. When this file is unclear, ask before guessing.

Last updated: April 2026
Phase: Two

---

## The Belief

Privé Padel believes a life well-lived is a life played well.

We exist for the part of the daily padel ritual that doesn't have a home — the trip, the new city, the long way somewhere. Our members already have the ritual. We are the membership that lets it travel with them.

This brand is for people already there. We do not need to call ourselves luxury. We let the destinations, the company, and the quality do the talking.

---

## The Ecosystem

The brand has a single architecture. All product, marketing, and communication should reflect this hierarchy.

**Privé Padel** — the master brand. Overarching.

  └── **Privé Passport** — the membership. The gateway to everything beneath.

      Member access includes:
      - **Privé Padel Locations** — flagship venues (Montauk, Privé Padel at THesis Miami)
      - **Padel Destinations** — 40+ partner resorts worldwide *(centerpiece offering)*
      - **Padel Experiences** — curated retreats, additional cost per experience
      - **Padel Club Partners** — urban club access in select cities, included with membership

The membership is the gateway. The four offerings are how members meet the world. All four belong to one membership because they belong to one life.

**Important:** Privé Padel locations (Montauk, Miami) are *both* flagship physical brand expressions *and* destinations within the Passport network. Public-facing surfaces should treat them as member-access venues alongside the partner network — not as a separate brand pillar.

## The Three Taglines

Three lines, three jobs. Each tagline maps to a specific use. They never compete with each other because they operate at different altitudes.

**WORLDVIEW — `A life played well.`**
- Used with: Privé Padel master brand
- Used in: manifesto moments, founding documents, the about page, deep editorial
- Register: slow, declarative, certain
- Never used as: button copy, hero CTA, social caption

**PRODUCT — `The game travels with you.`**
- Used with: Privé Passport
- Used in: product pages, hero imagery, member acquisition, the membership pitch
- Register: direct, warm, plain-spoken

**EDITORIAL — `Play the world.` / `The world, played.`**
- Used with: campaigns
- Used in: social, hero imagery, partner storytelling, travel-led campaigns
- "Play the world" is forward-looking (here's what awaits you). "The world, played" is reflective (look at what we've done together). Either is acceptable depending on the moment.

**Retired:** Tagline lines from previous phases ("The world's finest courts are waiting", "Privé Padel. Everywhere.", "Where padel meets destination travel") should not appear in any new surface.

## Voice — One Voice, Three Registers

The brand has one voice. The same voice operates at three registers depending on context.

**Worldview register** — for the about page, manifesto moments, founding documents.
- Slow, declarative, certain
- Example: *"A life well-lived is a life played well."*

**Membership register** — for product pages, member-facing communications, the practical work of explaining what Privé does.
- Direct, warm, plain-spoken
- Example: *"The game travels with you. From Montauk to Mallorca, the membership keeps the game ready when you arrive."*

**Editorial register** — for social, hero imagery, partner storytelling.
- Evocative, sensory, place-led, never selling
- Example: *"The court is ready. The tide is in. Montauk is waiting."*

**Across all three registers:**
- Lead with place and atmosphere
- Use quiet confidence
- Evoke feeling and movement
- Speak to a life well-lived
- Do not lead with product specs
- Do not use hype or urgency
- Do not list features and benefits as the primary content
- Do not speak to athletic achievement or competition

## Forbidden Words

Some words make a brand sound like every other brand. Avoid them, except where they are doing precise work.

**Banned outright:**

| Word | Why | Alternative |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Exclusive | Implies the wrong kind of gatekeeping | Selective, or "for members" |
| Lifestyle | Sounds like a magazine column | A way of living |
| Wellness | We are not a spa | (do not substitute — remove) |
| Elevated | Table stakes; saying so admits it might not be | (do not substitute — remove) |
| Members-only | Gatekeeping language | "For members" |

**Restricted (acceptable in precise use, banned as filler):**

| Word | Acceptable Use | Forbidden Use |
|------|---------------|---------------|
| Premium / luxury | Describing partner properties as a category ("The Leading Hotels of the World") | Describing Privé itself as luxurious |
| Curated | Describing the actual work of curation ("Padel Experiences are curated retreats") | Generic upgrade word ("a curated portfolio") |
| Experience | The proper noun "Padel Experiences" (the offering) | Generic upgrade noun ("have an experience") |

**Closing principle:** We use the words our reader would use over dinner. Nothing more.

## Color System

| Name | Hex | Role | Application |
|------|-----|------|-------------|
| Navy | `#1B2A4A` | Primary | Primary background for hero applications |
| Dark | `#0E1923` | Neutral | Deep ground for editorial moments, manifesto |
| Gold | `#B8976A` | Accent only | Hairlines, eyebrow labels, key markers — never as a fill |
| Sand | `#D9CDB8` | Support | Quiet structural support, secondary backgrounds |
| Cream | `#F5F0E8` | Base | Editorial layouts, body content backgrounds |

**Application rules:**
- Navy and Dark dominate as primary backgrounds for hero applications
- Cream serves as the base for editorial layouts and body content
- Gold appears as accent only — hairlines, eyebrow labels, key markers — never as a fill region
- Sand provides quiet structural support
- Never introduce a sixth color. Never use accent gradients.

## Typography

The brand uses two typefaces. No third typeface is permitted under any circumstances.

**Display — Cormorant Garamond**
- Weight 300 with italic accents
- Used for: headlines, pull quotes, hero text, tagline application
- Italic is used for emphasis, often on the second clause of a two-part headline ("A life *played well.*")
- Never bolded above weight 500

**UI / Body — Jost**
- Weights 200–400, with wide tracking
- Used for: labels, body copy, navigation, eyebrow tags
- All-caps treatment: letter-spacing 0.25em or wider
- Never used for headlines or display purposes

**Hierarchy:**
- H1 / Display: Cormorant Garamond, 64–88px (depending on context)
- H2 / Section: Cormorant Garamond, 36–48px
- H3 / Subhead: Jost, 14–18px
- Body: Jost Light, 14–18px
- Eyebrow / Label: Jost Medium, 9–11px, all caps, letter-spacing 0.25em+, gold

**Stand-ins:** During production, Lora may be substituted for Cormorant Garamond and Poppins for Jost as visual approximations. These are not final — production must use the actual brand fonts.

## Logo System

Three marks comprise the system. Use the right mark for the moment.

1. **Primary Padel mark** — for Privé Padel brand expressions, hero imagery, signage, brand-only contexts
2. **Privé Passport wordmark** — for membership-specific use, product pages, member-facing surfaces
3. **Full lockup** (Padel | gold rule | Passport) — for partnership, system, and architecture contexts where both brands must appear together

**Tagline lockups exist for:**
- Padel mark + "A life played well" (worldview)
- Passport mark + "The game travels with you" (product)
- Passport mark + "Play the world" or "The world, played" (campaign)

**Color variants** (production files exist):
- Cream on Navy *(default brand application)*
- Navy on Cream *(for cream-background editorial)*
- White on transparent *(for use on dark photography)*
- Black on transparent *(for use on light photography, single-color print)*
- Gold on Navy *(ceremonial, sparingly used)*
- Cream on Dark *(for near-black backgrounds)*
- Gold on transparent *(for foiling, embossing, single-ink)*

## The People

Privé is for the player who has already built padel into their life.

They are in their thirties or older. They run companies, design things, manage portfolios, raise families. They have been to the places people travel to once and have started traveling to the places people return to. They have a club at home. A partner they play with most weeks. A racquet they replace before they need to.

They are the kind of person who chooses the better restaurant without making a thing of it. They do not need a membership to feel a certain way. They need one because the game keeps following them, and they would like the game to be ready when they arrive.

**This brand is not for:** the person who needs a membership to feel arrived. The competitive player who wants ranking points. The traveler who wants the room with the best deal. The status seeker who is always introducing themselves. They will be happier somewhere else.

## Surface Design Patterns

These patterns are observed in the existing Privé Passport member platform and should be the reference for any public-facing surface. The platform IS the brand. Public surfaces should look like a logged-out version of it.

### Destination cards
- Region/country in small Jost caps tracked, gold (e.g. "YUCATÁN, MEXICO")
- Property name large in Cormorant Garamond serif (e.g. "Chablé Yucatán")
- Region descriptor beneath in Cormorant Italic, smaller (e.g. "Mayan Peninsula")
- Optional: small benefit tags as quiet outlined pills at the bottom (e.g. "2 COURTS · SACRED CENOTE SPA")
- Hero image full-bleed at top, content overlaid with subtle dark gradient
- Cards in horizontal carousels (4 visible, paginated 1/N)

### Collection framings
Collections should be presented as curatorial stories, not flat lists. Use editorial titles like:
- "Recently Added" / "New to the Network"
- "The Leading Hotels of the World Collection"
- "The Island Collection"
- "World's 50 Best Hotels Collection"
- "Featured Destination"

Each collection gets an eyebrow tag (Jost Medium small caps gold), a serif title with italic accent on the second clause, and a "View All Destinations" CTA at right.

### Hero / featured destination
- Full-bleed photography
- Eyebrow tag at top: "FEATURED DESTINATION" or similar
- Property name in two-line serif, italic on second word/clause
- Region in italic Cormorant beneath
- One short editorial paragraph (not a feature list)
- Benefit list as small dashed bullets (Preferred room rate, Room upgrade, etc.)
- Single primary CTA button (navy fill, cream text, no rounded corners)

### Header / navigation
- Slim navy band across the top
- Single brand mark left-aligned
- Maximum 4 nav items center
- Member affordance (initials in circle) right-aligned, or "Get Your Passport" CTA for public
- No dropdown menus on the primary nav unless absolutely necessary
- No promotional banners above the nav

### Section dividers
- Hairline gold rule, 60px wide, centered, often with eyebrow text above

### Stats / numbers (when used)
- Large number in Cormorant
- Tiny Jost label beneath in caps tracked
- Use sparingly. If data isn't ready, do not render zeroes — hide the section.

## Anti-patterns to remove

These exist on the current public website and should be eliminated:

- "The world's finest courts are waiting." (off-brand tagline)
- "Privé Padel. Everywhere." (off-brand tagline)
- Any use of "luxury" as a Privé self-description (use it only as a category descriptor for partner properties)
- "exclusively for Privé Passport members" (gatekeeping language)
- "Three ways to experience Privé Padel" (architecture is wrong — should be four offerings under one membership)
- The features-list treatment of Privé Passport benefits (replace with editorial place-first content)
- Stats showing zeroes (broken counters)
- Dropdown navigation menus (use clean linear nav)
- The current "Privé Clubs" section presented as parallel to Passport (clubs are a Passport offering, not a separate pillar)
